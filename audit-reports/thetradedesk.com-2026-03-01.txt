   ② No /llms.txt (0/12 pts)
     This is the dimension with the most room to grow. The Trade Desk sits
     at the intersection of programmatic advertising and AI — they have a
     Developer portal, UID2 documentation, Ventura ecosystem materials, and
     platform documentation that LLMs and buying agents would directly
     benefit from having indexed. A well-structured llms.txt pointing to

     The single <nav> element has no aria-label, making it ambiguous on

   QUICK WINS  (implement in < 1 week)
   ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

   • Add an Organization JSON-LD schema to the homepage <head>
     Include: name, legalName, url, logo, description, foundingDate,
     sameAs (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Wikipedia, GitHub, Bloomberg), and
     contactPoint. This is a static JSON blob — no backend work required.
     Moves structured data score from 7/15 to ~12/15 (+5 pts overall).

   • Add twitter:card meta tag
     `<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image">` — one
     line. The other four Twitter tags are already correct. Without this,
     the social share card degrades to basic text on X/Twitter.

   • Add aria-label="Primary navigation" to the <nav> element
     Single attribute change. Instantly distinguishes the nav for agents
     traversing multiple pages with different nav structures.

   • Add role="contentinfo" to the footer container
     One attribute, zero design impact. Properly signals footer content
     to screen readers and agents.

   STRATEGIC  (higher effort, longer term)
   ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

   • Create /llms.txt covering the full content estate
     Priority sections to include: Platform overview (/our-demand-side-
     platform), Developer docs, UID2 documentation, Ventura ecosystem,
     News/Press releases, Careers. Given The Trade Desk's positioning as
     an AI-era advertising platform, this file would be noticed by the
     industry and referenced as a best-practice example.

   • Add Article/NewsArticle schema to press releases on the homepage
     The three news items already have h3 headings and links — wrapping
     them in NewsArticle JSON-LD (headline, datePublished, url, publisher)
     would give agents structured access to news content without having
     to parse HTML.

   • Refactor dropdown button labels
     Replace "click to open dropdown Company" with "Company" (the
     accessible role/expanded state conveys the rest). Reduces noise
     in the accessibility tree for agents parsing nav structure.
